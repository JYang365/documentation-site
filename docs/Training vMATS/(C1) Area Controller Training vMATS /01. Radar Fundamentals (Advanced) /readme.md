# Radar Fundamentals (Advanced)

This section builds upon S3 knowledge and introduces radar control in an en-route environment.

Area controllers utilise radar to manage aircraft across large volumes of airspace, often across multiple sectors.

---

## Key Differences from Approach Control

- Greater distances between aircraft
- Higher speeds and altitudes
- Longer-term planning required
- Increased reliance on coordination

---

## Surveillance in ACC

Controllers rely primarily on:
- Secondary Surveillance Radar (SSR)
- Mode C / Mode S for altitude verification

---

## Separation Standards

### Lateral Separation

- Standard: 5 NM
- May increase depending on sector and workload

---

### Vertical Separation

- Minimum: 1000 ft (RVSM airspace)
- 2000 ft where applicable (FL290 - FL410)

---

## Strategic vs Tactical Control

### Tactical Control
- Immediate conflict resolution
- Heading or level changes

### Strategic Control
- Planning conflicts minutes in advance
- Using routing and level allocation

---

## Key Principle

> Area control is about solving problems before they develop, not reacting once they occur.


# 6.3 RNAV (GNSS) Approach

RNAV approaches use satellite navigation to guide aircraft to the runway.

---

## Characteristics

- No ground-based navigation required
- May include vertical guidance (LNAV/VNAV)

---

## Controller Responsibilities

- Position aircraft for final approach fix (FAF)
- Ensure aircraft is established on correct track
- Issue approach clearance

---

## Differences from ILS

- No localiser or glide slope
- Greater reliance on onboard systems
- Less tolerance for poor positioning

---

## Key Considerations

- Accurate positioning is critical
- Avoid tight vectoring close to final


# 6.2 ILS Approach

The Instrument Landing System (ILS) is a precision approach providing lateral and vertical guidance.

---

## Components

- Localiser (lateral guidance)
- Glide slope (vertical guidance)

---

## Controller Responsibilities

Controllers shall:
- Vector aircraft onto the localiser
- Ensure correct intercept angle (20°–30°)
- Establish aircraft prior to glide slope intercept
- Ensure adequate spacing

---

## Example Flow

1. Downwind established
2. Base turn issued
3. Intercept heading given
4. Aircraft captures localiser
5. Cleared for ILS approach

---

## Key Considerations

- Avoid high intercept angles
- Ensure aircraft is below glide slope when intercepting
- Maintain stable spacing

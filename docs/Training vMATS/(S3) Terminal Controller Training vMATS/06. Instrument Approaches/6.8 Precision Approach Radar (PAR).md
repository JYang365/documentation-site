# 6.8 Precision Approach Radar (PAR)

Precision Approach Radar (PAR) is a surveillance-based precision approach in which the controller provides continuous heading and vertical guidance to the pilot.

PAR is typically used in:
- Military operations
- Low visibility conditions
- Situations where onboard navigation is limited

---

## Characteristics

- Controller provides both lateral and vertical guidance
- Continuous communication is required
- High controller workload
- No onboard glidepath guidance required

---

## Controller Responsibilities

Controllers shall:

- Positively identify the aircraft
- Position aircraft on final approach track
- Provide continuous and accurate instructions
- Monitor glidepath and centreline deviations
- Ensure safe separation from other traffic

---

## Talkdown Phases

### Initial Phase

- Aircraft positioned on final approach heading
- Descent clearance issued

---

### Intermediate Phase

- Regular heading corrections
- Glidepath guidance provided

Example:
"Turn left 5 degrees"
"On glidepath"
"Slightly above glidepath, correct"

---

### Final Phase

- Increased precision required
- Rapid corrections as necessary

Example:
"Right 2 degrees"
"On centreline"
"Approaching decision height"

---

## Phraseology

Controllers shall use concise and standardised instructions:

- "Turn left/right X degrees"
- "On centreline"
- "Slightly left/right of centreline"
- "On glidepath"
- "Above/below glidepath"
- "Begin descent"

---

## Decision Height

At decision height:

"At decision height"

The pilot shall:
- Continue visually if runway in sight, or
- Execute a missed approach

---

## Missed Approach

If required:

"Go around, fly runway heading, climb to [altitude]"

Controllers must ensure:
- Missed approach path is protected
- Separation is maintained

---

## Key Considerations

- High workload environment
- Requires precise and continuous monitoring
- Minimal tolerance for error

---

## Training Note

PAR requires additional training and should only be conducted by controllers assessed as competent in radar vectoring and sequencing.

# Airspace Structure

The Terminal Manoeuvring Area (TMA) is the airspace in which approach control provides radar services to arriving and departing aircraft.

---

## Purpose of the TMA

- Facilitate safe climb and descent
- Enable sequencing of arrivals
- Integrate departures with en-route traffic

---

## Key Components

### Control Zones (CTR)
- Surround the aerodrome
- Controlled by Tower (TWR)

### Terminal Manoeuvring Area (TMA)
- Controlled by Approach (APP)
- Extends above and beyond the CTR

---

## Standard Routes

### SIDs (Standard Instrument Departures)
- Used for departing aircraft
- Provide structured exit paths

### STARs (Standard Terminal Arrival Routes)
- Used for arrivals
- Deliver aircraft into the TMA

---

## Controller Considerations

- Traffic flows and runway configuration
- Interaction between arrivals and departures
- Coordination with adjacent sectors

---

## Key Principle

> The airspace structure supports the controller — it does not replace planning.

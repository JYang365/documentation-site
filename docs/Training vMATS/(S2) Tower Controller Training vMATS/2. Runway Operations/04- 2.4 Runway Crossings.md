# 2.4 Runway Crossings

Runway crossings require strict control as they involve aircraft or vehicles entering an active runway.

---

## 2.4.1 Before Issuing a Crossing Clearance

Before approving a runway crossing, the Tower controller must ensure:

- No aircraft is taking off  
- No aircraft is landing or on short final  
- No aircraft is lined up on the runway  
- The runway is fully protected  
- The crossing can be completed without delay  

---

## 2.4.2 Crossing Clearance

A crossing clearance may only be issued when the runway is confirmed safe.

Example:

```text
GFA789, cross runway 27, report vacated.
```

## 2.4.3 Hold Short Instruction

If the runway is not safe for crossing, a hold short instruction shall be issued.

Example:

```text
GFA789, hold short runway 27.
```
---

## 2.4.4 After Crossing

After crossing, the aircraft should report when clear of the runway.

Example:

```text
GFA789, runway vacated.
```

---

## 2.4.5 Key Principle

Runway crossings must only be approved when the runway is fully protected. If there is any doubt regarding runway safety, the clearance must not be issued.

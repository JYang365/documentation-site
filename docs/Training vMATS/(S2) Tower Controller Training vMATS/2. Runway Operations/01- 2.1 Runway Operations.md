# 2.1 Runway Operations

Runway operations are the primary responsibility of the Tower controller. Every clearance issued must ensure that the runway is safe and free from conflict.

---

## 2.1.1 Runway Status

A runway is considered occupied when:

- An aircraft is taking off or landing  
- An aircraft is lined up and waiting  
- An aircraft or vehicle is crossing the runway  
- A landing aircraft has not fully vacated  
- A departing aircraft has not become airborne or passed the departure end  

---

## 2.1.2 Controller Responsibilities

Before issuing any runway clearance, the Tower controller must:

- Check final approach is clear  
- Ensure no conflicting traffic is on the runway  
- Monitor aircraft vacating the runway  
- Maintain awareness of all aircraft near holding points  

---

## 2.1.3 Use of “Line Up and Wait”

Line up and wait may be used to improve efficiency but must only be issued when:

- The runway is safe to enter  
- No landing aircraft is at short final  
- The controller can maintain full awareness  

---

## 2.1.4 Key Principle

If there is any doubt regarding runway safety, **do not issue the clearance**.

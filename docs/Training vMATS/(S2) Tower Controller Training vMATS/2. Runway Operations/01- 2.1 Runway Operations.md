# 2.1 Runway Operations

Runway operations are the primary responsibility of the Tower controller. All runway clearances must ensure that the runway is safe, protected, and free from conflict.

---

## 2.1.1 Runway Status

A runway is considered occupied when:

- An aircraft is taking off or landing  
- An aircraft is lined up and waiting  
- An aircraft or vehicle is crossing the runway  
- A landing aircraft has not fully vacated  
- A departing aircraft has not become airborne and passed the departure end  

---

## 2.1.2 Controller Responsibilities

Before issuing any runway clearance, the Tower controller shall:

- Check final approach is clear  
- Ensure no conflicting traffic is on the runway  
- Monitor aircraft vacating the runway  
- Maintain awareness of all aircraft at holding points  

---

## 2.1.3 Line Up and Wait (LUAW)

Line up and wait may be used to improve efficiency, provided:

- The runway is safe to enter  
- No aircraft is on short final  
- The controller can maintain continuous situational awareness  

---

## 2.1.4 Example Phraseology

```text
GFA123, line up and wait runway 27.
GFA123, hold short runway 27.
GFA123, runway 27, cleared for takeoff.
GFA456, runway 27, cleared to land.
```
---

## 2.1.5 Key Principle

Runway safety takes priority over efficiency. If there is any doubt regarding runway status or separation, a clearance must not be issued.

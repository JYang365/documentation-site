# 2.2 Takeoff and Landing Clearances

Takeoff and landing clearances are the most critical instructions issued by the Tower controller and must only be given when safe.

---

## 2.2.1 Takeoff Clearance

Before issuing takeoff clearance, ensure:

- The aircraft is at the correct runway or intersection  
- The runway is clear  
- Final approach is clear or safe spacing exists  
- Departure release has been obtained (if required)  
- Wake turbulence separation is considered  

### Example

```text
GFA123, wind 270 degrees 8 knots, runway 27, cleared for takeoff.
```

## 2.2.2 Landing Clearance

Before issuing landing clearance, ensure:

- The aircraft is established on the correct approach  
- The runway is clear or will be clear in time  
- No aircraft or vehicles are entering or crossing the runway  
- Wake turbulence separation is applied  

Example:

```text
GFA456, wind 270 degrees 8 knots, runway 27, cleared to land.
```
---

## 2.2.3 Go-Around

A go-around shall be instructed when:

- The runway is occupied  
- Separation cannot be maintained  
- The approach becomes unstable  
- Any unsafe situation develops  

Example:

```text
GFA456, go around, I say again go around.
```
---

## 2.2.4 Key Principle

Takeoff and landing clearances must only be issued when the runway is safe and separation is assured. If there is any doubt, the clearance must not be given.

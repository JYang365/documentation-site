## 6.1 General Principles

Emergency and abnormal situations require immediate, decisive action to maintain safety. The Tower controller must prioritise the affected aircraft while maintaining awareness of all other traffic.

The controller shall:

- Prioritise safety above all other considerations  
- Provide maximum assistance to the aircraft in distress  
- Maintain clear, concise, and calm communication  
- Avoid unnecessary frequency congestion  
- Coordinate with all relevant units without delay  

The controller must remain composed, structured, and proactive at all times.

---

### Controller Priorities

In handling an emergency, the order of priority is:

1. Aircraft in distress or emergency  
2. Aircraft with abnormal situations  
3. Other airborne traffic  
4. Departures and ground movements  

---

### Key Principles

- Do not delay action waiting for perfect information  
- Use plain language if standard phraseology is insufficient  
- Keep transmissions short and clear  
- Continuously reassess the situation  
- Anticipate the next required action  

---

### Example

```text
GFA123, roger MAYDAY, runway 27 cleared to land, wind 270 degrees 8 knots, emergency services are standing by.
```

# 1.2 Tower Controller Role

The Tower controller is responsible for the safe, orderly, and expeditious flow of air traffic on and in the vicinity of the aerodrome.

---

## 1.2.1 Core Responsibilities

The Tower controller shall:

- Issue clearances for takeoff and landing  
- Maintain runway separation between aircraft  
- Manage aircraft in the circuit  
- Control runway crossings  
- Provide traffic information as required  

---

## 1.2.2 Area of Responsibility

The Tower controller is responsible for:

- The active runway(s)  
- Aircraft on final approach (once transferred from Approach)  
- Aircraft in the visual circuit  
- Aircraft at holding points  
- Vehicles and aircraft crossing the runway  

---

## 1.2.3 Priorities

The controller must prioritise:

1. Aircraft on final approach  
2. Aircraft landing or taking off  
3. Aircraft in the circuit  
4. Departures  
5. Ground movements  

Safety always takes priority over efficiency.

---

## 1.2.4 Situational Awareness

The controller must maintain continuous awareness of:

- Runway occupancy  
- Aircraft positions  
- Traffic sequence  
- Potential conflicts  

Loss of situational awareness is a primary cause of controller error.

---

## 1.2.5 Decision Making

The Tower controller must:

- Make timely decisions  
- Act proactively rather than reactively  
- Avoid unnecessary delays  
- Maintain safe separation at all times  

---

## 1.2.6 Communication

The controller must use:

- Standard ICAO phraseology  
- Clear and concise instructions  
- Appropriate tone and pace  

Incorrect or unclear communication can lead to unsafe situations.

---

## 1.2.7 Key Principle

The primary duty of the Tower controller is safety. Efficiency must never compromise safe separation or correct procedure.

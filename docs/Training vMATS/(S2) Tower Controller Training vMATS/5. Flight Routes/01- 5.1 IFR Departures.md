# 5.1 IFR Departures

IFR departures are aircraft departing under Instrument Flight Rules. Tower controllers must ensure that departing IFR aircraft are correctly cleared, released where required, and safely sequenced with other runway traffic.

## Before Departure

Before issuing takeoff clearance, Tower should confirm that:

- The aircraft has received its IFR clearance.
- The assigned runway matches the clearance and current runway in use.
- The aircraft is at the correct holding point.
- Any required release has been obtained from Approach or the relevant radar position.
- The runway is clear and available.

## Departure Release

Some IFR departures may require a release before takeoff. If a release is required, Tower must not clear the aircraft for takeoff until the release has been obtained.

Example coordination:

```text
Tower: Request release GFA123, runway 27.
Approach: GFA123 released, no delay.
Tower: GFA123 released, no delay.
```

## Takeoff Clearance

When the aircraft is ready, released where required, and the runway is clear, Tower may issue takeoff clearance.

```text
GFA123, wind 270 degrees 8 knots, runway 27, cleared for takeoff.
```

## After Departure

After departure, Tower should transfer the aircraft to the appropriate radar or approach controller when safe and appropriate.

```text
GFA123, contact Departure 120.500.
```

## Key Point

Tower must ensure that IFR departures are correctly coordinated and safely sequenced with other runway traffic before issuing takeoff clearance.

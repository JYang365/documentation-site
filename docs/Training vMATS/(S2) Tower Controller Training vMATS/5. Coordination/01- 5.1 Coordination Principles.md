# 5.1 Coordination Principles

Coordination ensures the safe and efficient transfer of control and responsibility between controllers. The Tower controller must coordinate effectively with adjacent units at all times.

---

## 5.1.1 Objectives of Coordination

The controller aims to:

- Maintain safe separation between aircraft  
- Ensure smooth transfer of traffic between units  
- Prevent conflicting clearances  
- Maintain efficient traffic flow  

---

## 5.1.2 Responsibility Transfer

Responsibility for an aircraft transfers when:

- Communication is established with the next controller  
- The receiving controller accepts control  

Until transfer is complete, the original controller remains responsible.

---

## 5.1.3 Types of Coordination

Coordination at Tower level includes:

- Departure release coordination  
- Arrival coordination with Approach  
- Runway and traffic information exchange  
- Special or non-standard operations  

---

## 5.1.4 Coordination Methods

Coordination may be achieved by:

- Voice communication (primary method)  
- Text/coordination systems (where available)  
- Pre-defined procedures (LOAs or SOPs)  

All coordination must be clear and unambiguous.

---

## 5.1.5 Positive Coordination

Positive coordination means:

- Explicit agreement between controllers  
- No assumptions made  
- Both parties fully aware of the situation  

The controller must never assume coordination has occurred.

---

## 5.1.6 Standardisation

Standard procedures reduce workload and errors.

The controller should:

- Follow published SOPs and LOAs  
- Use standard phraseology  
- Avoid ad-hoc coordination unless necessary  

---

## 5.1.7 Situational Awareness

The controller must maintain awareness of:

- Traffic transferred to/from adjacent units  
- Pending departures awaiting release  
- Arrivals under Approach control  

Loss of awareness can result in coordination failures.

---

## 5.1.8 Error Prevention

To prevent coordination errors, the controller should:

- Confirm all critical coordination  
- Clarify any uncertainty immediately  
- Avoid rushing coordination under pressure  
- Re-check clearances before issuing  

---

## 5.1.9 Key Principle

Effective coordination is based on clarity, confirmation, and standardisation. Assumptions must never replace communication.

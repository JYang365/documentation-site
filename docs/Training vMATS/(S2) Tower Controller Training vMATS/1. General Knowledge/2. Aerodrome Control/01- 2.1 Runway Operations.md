# 2.1 Runway Operations

Runway operations are one of the main responsibilities of a Tower controller. Before issuing any takeoff, landing, crossing, or line-up instruction, the controller must ensure that the runway is safe and available.

## Runway Status

A runway is considered occupied when:

- An aircraft is taking off or landing.
- An aircraft is lined up and waiting.
- An aircraft or vehicle is crossing the runway.
- A landing aircraft has not fully vacated.
- A departing aircraft has not safely passed the departure end or become airborne.

## Tower Actions

Tower controllers should:

- Monitor aircraft approaching the runway.
- Check final approach before issuing takeoff clearance.
- Ensure landing aircraft have vacated before using the runway again.
- Avoid issuing conflicting runway clearances.
- Use clear instructions when aircraft are holding, lining up, crossing, or vacating.

## Example Phraseology

```text
GFA123, line up and wait runway 27.
GFA123, wind 270 degrees 8 knots, runway 27, cleared for takeoff.
GFA456, wind 270 degrees 8 knots, runway 27, cleared to land.
GFA789, hold short runway 27.

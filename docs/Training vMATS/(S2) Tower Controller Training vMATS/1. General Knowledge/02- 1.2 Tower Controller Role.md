# 1.2 Tower Controller Role

The Tower controller is responsible for runway operations and aircraft operating in the immediate vicinity of the aerodrome.

## Main Responsibilities

Tower controllers are responsible for:

- Issuing takeoff and landing clearances.
- Managing aircraft entering, crossing, or vacating the runway.
- Sequencing arriving and departing traffic.
- Controlling aircraft in the visual circuit.
- Providing traffic information where required.
- Coordinating with Ground and Approach controllers.

## Tower Control Area

The exact Tower area of responsibility depends on local procedures. In general, Tower controls:

- Active runways.
- Aircraft at runway holding points.
- Aircraft cleared to line up.
- Aircraft on final approach once transferred by Approach.
- Aircraft in the visual circuit.
- Aircraft after landing until transferred to Ground.

## Controller Priorities

Tower controllers should prioritise:

1. Runway safety.
2. Aircraft separation and sequencing.
3. Clear and accurate instructions.
4. Efficient traffic flow.
5. Coordination with adjacent positions.

# 4.4 Circuit Procedures

Circuit procedures allow aircraft to remain close to the aerodrome for training, visual approaches, or repeated takeoffs and landings.

Tower controllers must be able to manage aircraft in the circuit while also handling arrivals, departures, and runway movements.

## Circuit Positions

Common circuit positions include:

- Upwind
- Crosswind
- Downwind
- Base
- Final

Pilots may be instructed to report specific positions to help Tower maintain awareness.

## Touch-and-Go

A touch-and-go allows an aircraft to land and take off again without stopping.

Example:

```text
GFA22, wind 270 degrees 8 knots, runway 27, cleared touch and go.
```

## Circuit Instructions

Tower may issue instructions such as:

```text
GFA22, join left downwind runway 27, report downwind.
GFA22, extend downwind, I will call base.
GFA22, orbit right for spacing.
GFA22, number two, follow traffic on final.
```

## Key Point

Circuit traffic can quickly increase Tower workload. Controllers should use clear reporting instructions, maintain awareness of aircraft positions, and plan ahead to avoid runway or circuit conflicts.

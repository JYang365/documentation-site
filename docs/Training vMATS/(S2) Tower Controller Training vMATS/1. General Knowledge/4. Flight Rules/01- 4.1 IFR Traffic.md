# 4.1 IFR Traffic

IFR traffic operates under Instrument Flight Rules and normally follows an assigned clearance, route, and level. Tower controllers must understand how IFR aircraft are handled during departure, arrival, and runway operations.

## IFR Departures

Before an IFR aircraft departs, Tower should ensure that:

- The aircraft has received an IFR clearance.
- The departure runway is correct.
- Any required release has been obtained.
- The runway is clear.
- The aircraft is ready for departure.

## Takeoff Clearance

Once the runway is available and any required coordination is complete, Tower may issue takeoff clearance.

Example:

```text
GFA123, wind 270 degrees 8 knots, runway 27, cleared for takeoff.
```

## Key Point

Tower does not normally provide full radar separation for IFR aircraft, but Tower is responsible for safe runway use, correct clearances, and coordination before departure or landing.

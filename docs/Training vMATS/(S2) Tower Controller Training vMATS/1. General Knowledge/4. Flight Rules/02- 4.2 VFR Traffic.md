# 4.2 VFR Traffic

VFR traffic operates under Visual Flight Rules and is responsible for maintaining visual separation from other aircraft and terrain, unless local procedures state otherwise.

Tower controllers must understand how VFR aircraft operate in the circuit, join or leave the control zone, and interact with IFR traffic.

## Common VFR Operations

VFR aircraft may request:

- Startup and taxi.
- Circuit training.
- Departure from the control zone.
- Arrival into the aerodrome.
- Transit through controlled airspace.
- Touch-and-go, low approach, or full-stop landing.

## Tower Responsibilities

Tower should provide:

- Runway in use.
- Wind information.
- Circuit direction.
- Traffic information.
- Takeoff and landing clearances.
- Joining or reporting instructions.

## Example Phraseology

```text
GFA22, join left downwind runway 27, report downwind.
GFA22, number two, follow traffic on final.
GFA22, wind 270 degrees 8 knots, runway 27, cleared touch and go.
```

## Key Point

VFR aircraft may operate more flexibly than IFR aircraft, but Tower must still maintain runway safety, issue clear instructions, and provide traffic information where required.

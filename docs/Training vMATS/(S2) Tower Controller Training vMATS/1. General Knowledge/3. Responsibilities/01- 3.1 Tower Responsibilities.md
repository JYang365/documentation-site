# 3.1 Tower Responsibilities

The Tower controller is responsible for the safe and orderly movement of aircraft on runways and within the immediate vicinity of the aerodrome.

## Main Responsibilities

Tower controllers are responsible for:

- Issuing takeoff and landing clearances.
- Managing aircraft entering, crossing, lining up on, or vacating the runway.
- Sequencing arriving and departing traffic.
- Monitoring aircraft on final approach.
- Controlling aircraft in the visual circuit.
- Coordinating with Ground, Approach, and other relevant positions.
- Providing traffic information where required.

## Runway Safety

Tower must ensure that the runway is safe before issuing any runway clearance.

A takeoff or landing clearance should not be issued if:

- Another aircraft is occupying the runway.
- An aircraft or vehicle is crossing the runway.
- A previous landing aircraft has not vacated.
- A previous departure has not safely departed.
- There is uncertainty about the position of traffic.

## Situational Awareness

Tower controllers must maintain awareness of:

- Aircraft holding short of the runway.
- Aircraft lined up on the runway.
- Aircraft on final approach.
- Aircraft in the circuit.
- Aircraft that have landed and are vacating.
- Aircraft or vehicles crossing the runway.

## Coordination

Tower should coordinate with other controllers when required, especially for:

- Departure releases.
- Runway crossings.
- Runway changes.
- Missed approaches.
- Emergency traffic.
- Traffic affecting another controller’s area of responsibility.

## Key Point

Tower controllers must prioritise runway safety above all other tasks. Efficient traffic flow is important, but it must never come at the cost of safety.
